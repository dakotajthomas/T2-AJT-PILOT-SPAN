DIRECTIONS: Sentence Intuitions

Speakers of a language seem to develop a feel for what is a possible sentence, even in many cases where they have never been taught any particular rules.

For example, in Spanish, you may feel that sentences 1-3 below sound like possible Spanish sentences, while sentence 4 does not.

1) Hablo español cada día.
2) Isabela y yo hablamos español cada día.
3) Isabela habla español cada día.
4) Isabela y yo hablas español cada día.

Although sentences 2 and 4 are of the same structure, one can judge without depending on any rule that sentence 4 is impossible in Spanish.

Likewise, in English, you might feel that the first sentence below sounds like it is a possible English sentence, while the second one does not.

1) John is likely to win the race.
2) John is probably to win the race.
